The Kenya Athlete Association (KAA) proudly collaborates with key sporting bodies in football, swimming, rugby, athletics, and cricket to provide comprehensive support for athletes across multiple disciplines. Through strategic partnerships with national federations, clubs, and training academies, we ensure that athletes have access to top-tier coaching, high-performance training facilities, and career development opportunities. Our joint initiatives focus on talent identification, mentorship programs, and financial support to help athletes excel at both national and international levels. Additionally, we work closely with these associations to advocate for fair policies, athlete welfare, and career transition programs, ensuring long-term success both on and off the field. By uniting forces, we create a strong, supportive ecosystem that empowers Kenyan athletes to reach their highest potential.

Kenya Rugby Union

The Kenya Rugby Union (KRU) is the governing body responsible for overseeing the development and administration of rugby union in Kenya. Established in 1970, the KRU promotes both men's and women's rugby at various levels, from grassroots to the national team. It is known for fostering the growth of the sport in the country, with notable success in rugby sevens. The Kenya Sevens team, also known as Shujaa, has earned global recognition, participating in the World Rugby Sevens Series and winning the prestigious Singapore Sevens in 2016. Kenya Aquatics

Kenya Swimming Federation (KSF) is the governing body responsible for overseeing and promoting swimming activities in Kenya. It is affiliated with the International Swimming Federation (FINA) and the National Olympic Committee of Kenya (NOCK). KSF is tasked with developing swimming talent, organizing national competitions, and selecting athletes to represent Kenya in international events such as the Olympics, World Championships, and African Swimming Championships. The federation also works to improve coaching standards, enforce regulations, and enhance the overall growth of aquatic sports in the country.
