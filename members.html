Members

The Kenya Athlete Association (KAA) proudly collaborates with key sporting bodies in football, swimming, rugby, athletics, and cricket to provide comprehensive support for athletes across multiple disciplines. Through strategic partnerships with national federations, clubs, and training academies, we ensure that athletes have access to top-tier coaching, high-performance training facilities, and career development opportunities. Our joint initiatives focus on talent identification, mentorship programs, and financial support to help athletes excel at both national and international levels. Additionally, we work closely with these associations to advocate for fair policies, athlete welfare, and career transition programs, ensuring long-term success both on and off the field. By uniting forces, we create a strong, supportive ecosystem that empowers Kenyan athletes to reach their highest potential.

Kenya Rugby Union

The Kenya Rugby Union (KRU) is the governing body responsible for overseeing the development and administration of rugby union in Kenya. Established in 1970, the KRU promotes both men's and women's rugby at various levels, from grassroots to the national team. It is known for fostering the growth of the sport in the country, with notable success in rugby sevens. The Kenya Sevens team, also known as Shujaa, has earned global recognition, participating in the World Rugby Sevens Series and winning the prestigious Singapore Sevens in 2016. Kenya Aquatics

Kenya Swimming Federation (KSF) is the governing body responsible for overseeing and promoting swimming activities in Kenya. It is affiliated with the International Swimming Federation (FINA) and the National Olympic Committee of Kenya (NOCK). KSF is tasked with developing swimming talent, organizing national competitions, and selecting athletes to represent Kenya in international events such as the Olympics, World Championships, and African Swimming Championships. The federation also works to improve coaching standards, enforce regulations, and enhance the overall growth of aquatic sports in the country. Athletics Kenya

Athletics Kenya (AK) is the governing body responsible for overseeing and developing athletics in Kenya. Established in 1951, the organization regulates track and field, road running, cross country, and other athletics disciplines within the country. AK plays a crucial role in nurturing talent, organizing national competitions, and selecting athletes for international events such as the Olympics, World Championships, and the Commonwealth Games. Football Kenya Federation

Football Kenya Federation (FKF) is the governing body responsible for overseeing and developing football in Kenya. Affiliated with FIFA and the Confederation of African Football (CAF), FKF manages national teams, organizes local leagues, and promotes grassroots football. It oversees the Kenyan Premier League, FKF Cup, and various youth development programs to nurture talent. Despite challenges such as governance issues and financial constraints, FKF continues working to improve football standards in the country. Cricket Kenya

Cricket Kenya is the governing body for cricket in Kenya, responsible for the administration, development, and promotion of the sport across the country. It is affiliated with the International Cricket Council (ICC) as an associate member and is also a part of the Africa Cricket Association. Kenya has a rich cricketing history, with its national team achieving significant success, particularly in the early 2000s, when they reached the semi-finals of the 2003 ICC Cricket World Cup. However, in recent years, the sport has faced challenges, including administrative issues and declining performances.
